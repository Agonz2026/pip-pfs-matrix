PIP — Proposal for Settlement (PFS) Strategy Matrix

New-law cases (no statutory one-way attorney's fees) — Florida §768.79 / Rule 1.442

How this works — the 80% principle (click to collapse)

Why it matters: For new-law PIP cases (HB 837, policies issued/renewed on or after 3/24/2023), the one-way attorney-fee statute no longer guarantees fees to a prevailing insured. A plaintiff's §768.79 Proposal for Settlement is the primary path to fees, so the offer amount has to be set deliberately.

The rule: If the defendant rejects your proposal and you obtain a judgment at least 25% more than the offer, you recover fees and costs incurred from the date the offer was served.

The math: "Judgment ≥ 1.25 × Offer" is the same as "Offer ≤ 80% of the expected judgment." So 80% of your expected judgment is the ceiling for a safe PFS — shown as Max Safe Offer. The Recommended Offer drops below that by your safety buffer so you don't have to win to the penny to trigger fees. Keep Threshold Status on SAFE; FAIL means the offer is too high to ever earn fees.

Blue cells = your inputs  •  black cells = auto-calculated  •  EV — Litigate = P(win) × (Judgment + recoverable PFS fees) − P(lose) × your unrecoverable trial costs.

Internal decision-support only — not legal advice. Verify the current statute, Rule 1.442, good-faith/specificity case law in your venue, and that the policy is genuinely post-3/24/2023 before serving any proposal.
